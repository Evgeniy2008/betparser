How to bet on soccer - Soccer betting explained

Soccer is typically played by two teams consisting of 11 players each (not including substitutes). The standard time of play for soccer is 90 minutes, with the addition of injury time and extra time increasing this total depending on the match format. The primary objective of soccer is to shoot the ball into the opponents’ goal to score points. The team with the most points at the end of the match is the winner. However, draws can also occur if both teams score the same amount of goals.

The Premier League is considered the most prestigious soccer league in the world, with iconic soccer stars like Harry Kane, Cristiano Ronaldo, Jack Grealish, Mason Mount and others having competed in the league.

Money Line - Match Outright - Soccer Money Lines

A Money Line bet is common in soccer, with it being a bet on the winning team of a match. Generally, the odds on a Money Line bet reflect which team is viewed as the favorite and which is the underdog. As there is the possibility of a draw in soccer, it is also an option to bet on the match being a draw. You may also find that this market is referred to as '1X2'.

Totals betting - Goals scored

Totals betting in soccer is a bet on the total number of goals scored in a match. There are a number of variations on this betting market depending on the match, which include Team Total, Total - 1st Half, Total Goals Odd/Even and more.

Handicap betting - Soccer spreads

In betting terms, a handicap is a virtual advantage or disadvantage given to a team to make the matchup between the two teams more even from an odds perspective. It is typically denoted in plus (+) or minus (-). A positive handicap in soccer indicates that the team is viewed as the underdog, while a negative implies that the team is favored. Typically, handicaps are given in 0.5 increments, e.g. Man United (+1.5).

Asian Handicap

The Asian Handicap is a unique betting market in soccer in which there are three variations: Level, Single and Split Handicap.

Correct Score betting

Correct Score betting is a prediction on the exact score of a match. While more difficult to predict, soccer prediction models like xG (expected goals) provide an excellent starting point for increasing the accuracy of your Correct Score betting predictions.

Corners betting

Corners betting in soccer is a unique market to bet on, as it provides an interesting way for bettors to interact with soccer matches outside of some of the more common betting markets like the Money Line and Total markets. Pinnacle provides two Corners betting options, Totals and Handicap.

Popular soccer leagues

Being one of the most popular sports in the world, soccer has a wide range of domestic and international soccer leagues ranging from the top end with the English Premier League (EPL) to smaller leagues like Japan's J1 League and South Korea's K League. Pinnacle offers a variety of soccer odds for tournaments and leagues around the world.

English Premier League (EPL)

The English Premier League, colloquially known as just the 'Premier League', is considered the best soccer league in the world, attracting top soccer talent and billions of viewers from across the globe. The EPL is one of the most popular soccer leagues for soccer bettors, with the EPL season taking place between August and May.

UEFA Champions League

The UEFA Champions League is an international soccer tournament featuring soccer clubs from around Europe competing for the European Champion Clubs' Cup and the prestigious title of champions. The UEFA Champions League is one of the few instances where teams from the English Premier League may encounter opponents from other large soccer leagues like Spain's LaLiga, home to teams like Real Madrid and Barcelona, or Germany's Bundesliga, which is home to Bayern Munich and Borussia Dortmund.

LaLiga

The Campeonato Nacional de Liga de Primera División, commonly known as LaLiga, is the premier soccer league in Spain, with globally recognized teams such as Real Madrid and Barcelona competing in the league. Founded in 1929, some of the most recognizable names in soccer, including Lionel Messi, have competed in LaLiga. Similar to other soccer leagues, LaLiga operates a promotion and relegation system which makes each season of the competition an exciting affair.

Bundesliga

The Bundesliga is Germany's top soccer league, and is amongst the most popular in the world. Established in 1963, there are 18 teams competing in the latest season of the league. Bayern Munich are the most decorated club in the Bundesliga with 32 titles (as of 2023), and the league has had multiple teams go on to have international success at tournaments like the UEFA Champions League.

Serie A

Serie A is Italy's top flight soccer league, and is both one of the oldest and most popular in the world. Founded in 1898, the Serie A is home to 20 famous clubs like Juventus, Inter Milan and AC Milan who all compete for the Coppa Campioni d'Italia.

Ligue 1

Ligue 1 is the top league in French soccer and consists of 18 teams. Ligue 1 is best known for its most successful club, Paris Saint-Germain, who have won 11 Ligue 1 championships (as of 2023). Running under a similar schedule to the EPL, the Ligue 1 season takes place between August and May.

In-play soccer betting - Live soccer odds

Live soccer betting has become an increasingly popular way for soccer bettors to get involved in the action. There are a number of live soccer markets that are available, from Money Line to Totals, and more. As a soccer match plays out, odds are updated to reflect the state of the game, offering bettors the opportunity to bet on their favorite team as they watch the match.
